<b>Abstract:</b>
The popular approach to hardness vs randomness shifted in the last five years. Instead of starting from circuit lower bounds and building PRGs, many recent works start from hardness assumptions for uniform algorithms, and build objects called targeted PRGs. This shift led to a surprisingly large number of new results and research directions.
<br><br>
In the talk we'll first see what exactly is new in this recent approach, and mention some technical tools that have been constructed and are now available off-the-shelf. The main focus will then be applications, ranging across derandomization, explicit constructions, circuit lower bounds, low-space algorithms, arithmetic complexity, and (of course) cryptography -- specifically, a close connection to Fiat-Shamir.
