<b>Abstract:</b>
Lossy kernelization has emerged as a powerful preprocessing paradigm in parameterized approximation, but existing approaches are largely limited to problems with a single optimization objective. In this talk, we introduce a general framework for lossy kernelization in multi-criteria optimization settings, where multiple constraints must be handled simultaneously. We demonstrate the applicability of this framework via Fair and Matroid Constrained Max k-Weight SAT—a natural generalization of Maximum Coverage that incorporates both fairness constraints and matroid independence.
